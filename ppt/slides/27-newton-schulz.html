27 · HYBRID NEWTON-SCHULZ
CHAPTER 04 · TWO-STAGE 10-STEP ITERATION

The first 8 steps drag singular values toward 1, the last 2 lock them in

A full SVD would be too expensive, so Newton-Schulz approximates the projection with polynomial iteration. V4’s hybrid version splits the 10 steps into two phases with different coefficients.
Illustration · all singular values converge to 1 within 10 steps, approaching from both the high and low sides
Newton-Schulz is a matrix-multiplication-only polynomial iteration. Each step replaces M with a combination like aM + b(MMᵀ)M + c(MMᵀ)^2M. With the right coefficients, the singular values move toward 1 without ever computing a real SVD.
PHASE 1 · STEPS 1-8
Push hard: drag singular values near 1 quickly
(a, b, c) = (3.4445, -4.7750, 2.0315)
The coefficients are aggressive, so values far from 1 get corrected violently. Overshoot is acceptable here because later steps will clean it up.
PHASE 2 · STEPS 9-10
Refine: pin the values exactly at 1
(a, b, c) = (2, -1.5, 0.5)
These coefficients are gentler and more monotonic. They stabilize the endgame and remove the small oscillations left over from Phase 1.
