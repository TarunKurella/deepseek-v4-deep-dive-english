59 · The Real Value of 1M
CHAPTER 09 · THESIS 1 · THE TRUTH OF ACCESSIBILITY

1M context is not about “being usable,” it means you stop rationing words

Before, you carefully decided which chunk to feed the model. Now you can dump in whatever you want, and the planning step itself becomes unnecessary.
BEFORE · THE 128K ERA
Every token counted
Open a codebase and your first question is “which files matter most, how should I chunk them, and do I need RAG?”

You are doing planning and triage for the model, not solving the problem. Every context window feels like calculating the fare before calling a cab.
→
AFTER · 1M AS THE DEFAULT
Throw in as much as you want
Drop in the whole repo, keep a week of chat history, read 30 PDFs at once.

You no longer save words for the context window. The whole act of planning around scarcity disappears, like moving from fare-by-fare taxis to an unlimited transit pass.
The real value of 1M context is not a number like “8x more capacity.” It is that a front-loaded expert judgment step becomes a default step you can simply skip. That is what accessibility actually means here.
