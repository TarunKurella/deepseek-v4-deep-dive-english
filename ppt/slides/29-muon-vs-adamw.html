Same data, same compute, Muon gets there first

The V4 paper does not publish a full head-to-head training curve, so this slide is a synthesis based on the original Muon papers and the V4 training description.
Illustration · Muon is smoother and drops faster · the paper does not disclose the full training curve, so this is directional
V4 TEAM’S TAKEAWAY
Faster convergence, more stable training
FASTER
Convergence speed
To reach the same loss level, Muon needs noticeably fewer training tokens than AdamW. The V4 paper explicitly lists this as a core reason for the optimizer swap.
SMOOTHER
Training stability
Orthogonalization stops dominant directions from monopolizing the update. Loss fluctuations calm down, which matters even more in large-scale MoE training.
NO QK-CLIP
One fewer supporting trick
V4 applies RMSNorm to both query and KV, so attention logits do not explode. That removes the need for the QK-Clip trick used in the original Muon paper.
Scope note: embeddings, the prediction head, static bias terms, and RMSNorm still use AdamW; the remaining modules switch to Muon. This is an engineering compromise: keep the proven old optimizer on the small pieces, switch the heavy machinery to the new one.
