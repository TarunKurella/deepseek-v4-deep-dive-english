CHAPTER 05 · INFRASTRUCTURE

Every seam got squeezed: six infrastructure modules

Section 3 is the most infrastructure-heavy part of the whole report. DeepSeek went through training and inference line by line, tightening every engineering seam. These six modules are the entry points into that checklist.
01
COMMUNICATION
MoE comm
compute overlap
Split the MoE layer into four stages and schedule them in waves for fine-grained pipelining. Theoretical 1.92× acceleration, with RL rollout peaking at 1.96× in practice.
02
DSL
TileLang
domain-specific language
Fuse kernels with TileLang. Host codegen cuts CPU-side validation from tens of microseconds to below 1 microsecond. Integer analysis is assisted by an SMT solver.
03
DETERMINISM
Batch-invariant
deterministic kernels
Training and inference become bit-exact. Dual-kernel attention, independent accumulation buffers, and DeepGEMM replace cuBLAS for reproducible matmul behavior.
04
QUANTIZATION
FP4 quantization-aware
training
MoE weights and indexer QK both go to FP4. FP4→FP8 dequantization stays lossless, top-k selection gets a 2× speedup, and KV recall stays at 99.7%.
05
TRAIN FRAMEWORK
Training framework
three key tricks
Muon × ZeRO uses knapsack-style bucketing, mHC wall-time overhead is driven down to 6.7%, and tensor-level checkpointing automates recomputation.
06
INFERENCE FRAMEWORK
Inference framework
heterogeneous KV management
A two-level State Cache + KV Cache pool manages heterogeneous attention state. Shared-prefix KV can even spill to disk to reduce first-token latency for long-context agents.
