CHAPTER 02 · THE MATH OF MHC

Two constraints define the doubly stochastic matrix

Doubly Stochastic Constraint
W ∈ ℝn×n,   Wij ≥ 0
∑j Wij = 1  (each row sums to 1)
∑i Wij = 1  (each column sums to 1)
⟹ ‖W‖2 ≤ 1    bounded spectral norm
⟹ W1 · W2   is still doubly stochastic
INSIGHT 01 · Why constrain row sums = 1
Inbound conservation
The total incoming signal weight at each downstream node stays fixed at 1,
so combined contributions cannot blow up without bound.
INSIGHT 02 · Why constrain column sums = 1
Outbound conservation
The total outgoing signal weight from each upstream node also stays fixed at 1,
so no single path can monopolize the energy.
INSIGHT 03 · What the two together imply
Depth becomes naturally stable
Every residual transform stays on this manifold,
so even 61 layers of multiplication neither amplify nor decay the signal.
