05 · INFRA · PHILOSOPHY
SUMMARY · THE ENGINEERING PHILOSOPHY OF THE INFRA CHAPTER

Three principles, running through the whole chapter

01
Do not eat the hardware vendor’s free lunch
Write the critical layers yourself, then push requirements back onto the hardware vendor.
  • MegaMoE, DeepGEMM, and TileLang are all in-house
  • “6144 FLOPs/Byte” is effectively a hardware design requirement written back to NVIDIA
02
Reproducibility is a first-class feature
Training and inference stay aligned, so debugging stops being mystical.
  • Batch invariance + deterministic kernels + TileLang numeric control
  • Together, those let a loss spike be reproduced precisely instead of guessed at
03
Be stingy with every microsecond and every byte
This is not “we propose X” research language. It is engineering craft built by clearing bottleneck after bottleneck.
  • Host codegen saves tens of microseconds; Muon BF16 compression saves half the communication
  • Disk KV reuses prefixes; mHC recomputation is applied only where it pays off
