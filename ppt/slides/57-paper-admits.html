CHAPTER 08 · HONEST ADMISSIONS

Three limitations the paper openly admits

Section 6, Limitations. DeepSeek says the uncomfortable parts out loud, which is unusually rare in domestic model papers.
01
ARCHITECTURAL BLOAT
The architecture is overloaded with components, and it is unclear which ones truly matter
“The current architecture stacks many components, mHC, HCA, CSA, DTA, Muon, and LPO, and we do not yet provide a systematic ablation of each component’s independent contribution. More rigorous decomposition studies are needed.”
02
EMPIRICAL, NOT UNDERSTOOD
Some key techniques work, but the team does not yet understand why
“Anticipatory Routing and SwiGLU Clamping are empirically effective, but we lack a theoretical understanding of their underlying mechanisms. Why they work and when they fail remain open questions.”
03
SPARSITY NOT EXTREME ENOUGH
The sparse design can likely be pushed further; the activation ratio is not yet minimal
“We believe there is still room for further sparsification. The current 49B/1.6T configuration, with a 3% activation ratio, is still not at the theoretical limit. More extreme sparsity would worsen routing imbalance and training instability, but may also be the next key lever for open models to beat closed ones.”
This is not vague humility. These are three concrete, falsifiable, technically interrogable admissions. “The most honest model” is not just branding here.
