Chapter 1 · Training stability

How does a 1.6T model avoid blowing up? Two life-saving tricks that work even if the theory is incomplete.

TRICK 01
Anticipatory Routing
route correction ahead of spikes
When loss suddenly spikes, the routing index can get dragged off course. V4 re-estimates routing using historical parameters from Δt steps earlier, and only triggers it when a spike appears.

Cost: roughly 20% routing overhead, but nearly negligible when amortized over the full run.
TRICK 02
SwiGLU Clamping
cap the gate before it explodes
The linear term in SwiGLU can occasionally jump to extreme values and destabilize numerics. V4 takes a blunt but effective approach: clamp the linear term to [-10, 10], and cap the gate term at 10.

Tradeoff: in theory it truncates gradient signal, but in practice the stability win is worth far more.
