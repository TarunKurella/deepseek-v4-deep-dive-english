28 · RMS RESCALE
CHAPTER 04 · NO HYPERPARAMETER RE-SWEEP

Change the optimizer, not the learning rate

Muon update magnitudes live on a different scale from AdamW. Normally that would force a fresh learning-rate sweep. V4 adds one RMS rescale step to bring them back into the same regime.
WITHOUT RESCALE
Direct replacement means a full hyperparameter re-sweep
Ot = HybridNewtonSchulz(μMt-1 + Gt)
After orthogonalization, the RMS of the update matrix depends on the matrix dimensions. The larger n × m gets, the smaller the RMS becomes. That means the learning rate and weight decay tuned for AdamW no longer transfer directly.
Cost: a huge retuning job, starting at tens of thousands of GPU-hours
WITH RESCALE · V4’S APPROACH
Multiply by √max(n,m) · γ
Ot = O′t · √max(n, m) · γ
This explicitly rescales the orthogonalized update back toward the same magnitude AdamW would have produced. As a result, hyperparameters like η and λ can reuse AdamW-era tuned values instead of being rediscovered from scratch.
Benefit: reuse V3-era tuning knowledge and start training immediately
This looks like a small detail, but it is critical in practice: it turns “change optimizer” from a research detour into a plug-in replacement. At thousand-GPU scale, that shortcut removes an entire path from the schedule.
