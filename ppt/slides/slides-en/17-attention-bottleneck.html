CHAPTER 03 · HYBRID ATTENTION CSA + HCA

Once context gets long, two walls hit you at once

The compute wall and the memory wall both explode as functions of n. That is the real reason why “native 1M tokens” is hard to make practical.
Illustrative curves · under native 1M context, both ceilings arrive at once
BOTTLENECK · 1
O(n²) compute
In standard self-attention, every query compares against every key. Double the context and FLOPs quadruple. Going from 4K to 1M is roughly a sixty-thousand-fold jump in work.
BOTTLENECK · 2
O(n) memory
For every layer and every token, K and V must stay in memory for later queries. Multiply one million tokens by dozens of layers and the KV cache eats the whole machine.
The next few slides answer one question: if you cannot keep everything, what do you keep and how? V4’s answer is to split attention into two kinds and let each handle a different side of the problem.
